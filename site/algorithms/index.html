How runtime scales with resolution

Mean wall-clock time over 50 runs, log–log axes. Watch Ultra-Fast CVT stay flat while the tree-based methods (ANN-FQ, KD-ANN-FQ) and EM/GMM climb steeply.

Mean computational time vs R

log–log · seconds · Nsim = 3,000 · N = 50 · niter ≤ 100

R-independence is the whole game. For R > 1024, KN runs ~1.5 orders of magnitude faster than LX, and Ultra-Fast CVT holds ~0.30 s regardless of resolution — while EM/GMM's O(R³) covariance step makes it the slowest above R = 512.
